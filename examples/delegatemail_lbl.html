LBNL Open Access FAQ:

Why should I do this?

It’s part of your job! DOE and Berkeley Lab policy require that all materials published by Berkeley Lab researchers as part of their employment at the Berkeley Lab or produced by using Berkeley Lab resources be submitted to DOE OSTI and made freely available to the public at no charge.

How do I use the system?

Here is a short training deck. We also have more comprehensive training materials.

What is the Open Access Policy?

LBNL has adopted an Open Access Policy in accordance with the Department of Energy Public Access Plan. Read our OA FAQ for more information.

What is the Publication Management System?

The LBNL Publication Management System is an automated system administered by the California Digital Library on behalf of LBNL in order to facilitate compliance with our OA policy. The system finds potentially relevant publications, presents them to authors for verification, stores bibliographic data and open access publications, and forwards them to OSI as required.

I need technical help.

We suggest you start with your division publication coordinator. If that person is unavailable, submit a help request or contact us.
