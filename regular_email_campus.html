About the UC Open Access Policy

UC’s Open Access policies ensure that research articles authored by UC authors at all 10 campuses of UC are made available to the public at no charge. The UC Libraries are working closely with the California Digital Library on implementation of the policies. Learn more about the Open Access Policies.

Make your work more discoverable online.

Verify your authorship of an article, then upload the final, post-peer review version of your manuscript. This makes your article freely available to the public on eScholarship, UC’s open access repository. Each article will receive a permalink, proper citation information and usage statistics.

Adding your manuscript to eScholarship significantly increases the reach and impact of your research and fulfills the terms of the UC Open Access Policy. The California Digital Library and UC Libraries have been charged with implementing the Policy.

What is the UC Publication Management System?

The UC Publication Management System is an automated system administered by the California Digital Library to support participation in the Open Access policy. The system scans a range of bibliographic databases to find your publications. If you choose to waive the rights under the UC Open Access Policy and not deposit your articles, you can still use the Publication Management System to collect and manage your publication information and create a digital CV.
